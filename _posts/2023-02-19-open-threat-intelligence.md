* VirusTotal (https://www.virustotal.com): A free online service that analyzes suspicious files and URLs for viruses and other malware.

* Shodan (https://www.shodan.io): A search engine for Internet-connected devices that allows you to identify vulnerabilities and security issues in systems.

* AlienVault Open Threat Exchange (https://otx.alienvault.com): A community-driven threat intelligence platform that allows users to share and analyze information about malicious activities and indicators of compromise.

* RiskIQ Community Edition (https://community.riskiq.com): A free tool that provides insights into threat actors, their tactics, techniques and procedures (TTPs), and their infrastructure.

* Censys (https://censys.io): A search engine that provides information about hosts and networks on the Internet, including SSL certificates and open ports.

* AbuseIPDB (https://www.abuseipdb.com): A free public IP address blacklist that collects and shares data on malicious IPs and their activities.

* IBM X-Force Exchange (https://exchange.xforce.ibmcloud.com): A cloud-based platform that provides threat intelligence feeds, including indicators of compromise, threat intelligence reports, and vulnerability data.

* Malware Domain List (https://www.malwaredomainlist.com): A comprehensive list of domains that are known to be associated with malware and other malicious activities.

* Shadowserver Foundation (https://www.shadowserver.org): A non-profit organization that collects, analyzes, and disseminates data on malicious activities, including botnets, malware, and phishing.

* National Vulnerability Database (https://nvd.nist.gov): A database that provides information about known vulnerabilities in software and hardware products, along with severity scores and other relevant details.

* OpenPhish (https://openphish.com): A database of known phishing websites, including their URLs and associated IPs.

* ThreatCrowd (https://www.threatcrowd.org): A search engine that aggregates data from various sources, including VirusTotal, AlienVault, and MalwareMustDie, to provide insights into threats and their infrastructure.

* Hybrid Analysis (https://www.hybrid-analysis.com): A free online service that analyzes suspicious files and URLs for malware and other malicious activities.

* IPinfo (https://ipinfo.io): A platform that provides information about IP addresses, including geolocation, hostname, and network owner.

* MISP (https://www.misp-project.org): A threat intelligence sharing platform that allows organizations to collaborate and share information about threats and indicators of compromise.

* GreyNoise Intelligence (https://www.greynoise.io): A platform that provides information about Internet background noise, including benign scanning activity and noisy IP addresses.

* Cyber Threat Alliance (https://www.cyberthreatalliance.org): A nonprofit organization that shares threat intelligence among its members to help protect against cyber threats.

* Recorded Future (https://www.recordedfuture.com): A threat intelligence platform that provides insights into threats and their associated indicators of compromise.

* SecurityTrails (https://securitytrails.com): A platform that provides information about domain and IP address histories, including DNS records and SSL certificates.

* DomainTools (https://www.domaintools.com): A platform that provides information about domain and IP address histories, including WHOIS records and related domains.
